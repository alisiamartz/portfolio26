<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>

At Volkswagen Group’s Electronic Research Lab / IECC, I developed VR/AR experiences to explore and communicate automotive concepts—from early exploration to later-stage visualization. I was the first VR Design Specialist at the ERL and helped establish the Mixed Reality lab as a long-term capability.

<h2 id="challenge">Challenge</h2>

This work is largely proprietary, so the story must focus on methods, systems, and prototyping practice rather than sensitive project details.

Technically, mixed reality is unforgiving: physical-to-virtual alignment, calibration, and reliability are make-or-break for believability.

<h2 id="approach">Approach</h2>

- Treat prototypes as systems: input → logic → feedback → iteration.
- Use physical mappings (levers/doors/seating rigs) to keep concepts grounded.
- Build repeatable infrastructure so the team can iterate quickly across projects.

<h2 id="process">Process</h2>

- Built VR interaction systems in Unity/C# using Vive and Leap Motion.
- Implemented mixed reality behaviors where physical levers/doors map to virtual cockpit elements.
- Implemented MQTT client/server communication between a vehicle and Unity to prototype system behavior.
- Created 2D instrument cluster simulations based on motion and visual designs from After Effects and Illustrator.
- Established and managed the ERL Mixed Reality lab, including budget allocation and hardware setup.

<h2 id="outcome">Outcome</h2>

The Mixed Reality lab became an ongoing ERL capability, enabling partners across multiple brands to evaluate interaction concepts earlier and with higher confidence—while keeping project specifics confidential.
