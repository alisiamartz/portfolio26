<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>

DUET is a **movement ritual for virtual reality**: a co-located experience designed for two participants at a time, experienced *in front of* an audience. In pairs, participants adorn headsets and are guided by **interactive music and light** through an immersive choreographic arc—both playful and meditative—where movement isn’t just expression, it’s the input that drives the world.

Rather than treating the headset as an isolating device, DUET reframes VR as a space for **embodied, collaborative interaction**—and invites a new relationship between performer and spectator, even when the “performers” are everyday participants.

<h2 id="challenge">Challenge</h2>

Co-located VR is socially and technically delicate—especially in a live setting. The experience needed to feel:
- **safe and human** (consent, comfort, clarity),
- **reliable in shared space** (alignment, timing, calibration),
- and **legible to witnesses** (a satisfying arc for the room, not just the headset).

<h2 id="approach">Approach</h2>

- Treat interaction as **choreography**: minimal vocabulary, clear prompts, strong pacing.
- Design for **paired intimacy + public spectacle**: what matters is the relationship between bodies in a space.
- Use **music/light as guidance** to keep the experience intuitive and emotionally coherent.
- Build for iteration: tune comfort, timing, and feedback until it reads as a “ritual,” not a “game loop.”

<h2 id="process">Process</h2>

- Prototyped the core two-person loop where **movement powers the experience**.
- Built and refined the “guided” layer (timing, cues, feedback) so the duet feels synchronized without being rigid.
- Iterated on co-located reliability and comfort details that matter in a live environment: onboarding, calibration, pacing, and fail-safes.
- Shaped an experience arc that **builds toward an impactful moment of physical connection**—earned, not forced.

<h2 id="outcome">Outcome</h2>

DUET became a repeatable foundation for exploring **embodied collaboration in VR**—one that shifts VR from a solitary medium into a shared, witnessed, and emotionally resonant experience. It also served as a proof point that co-located XR can support performance language: intimacy, timing, presence, and connection as first-class design goals.
